Overview

A Closed-Loop Timing and Risk Problem

At velocity v, observation-to-action delay tau creates roughly v tau meters of unobserved travel: at 10 m/s, 50 ms is already 0.5 m. Rendering, sensing, estimation, inference, actuation, and aerodynamic errors compound before the next correction.

Because a policy changes its future observations, visual realism alone cannot establish transfer. We will define task-relevant world edits, timing budgets, paired evaluation stages, and failure evidence for vision-conditioned agile flight.

Technical Thesis

AirSim, FlightGoggles, and Flightmare enabled scalable aerial simulation. NeRF/3DGS systems such as NeRF2Real, SplatSim, RE3SIM, VR-Robo, SOUS VIDE, Splat-Nav, GRaD-Nav++, and VISTA, plus recent Gaussian-splatting flight transfer and event-camera avoidance, now expose a sharper question: does simulated evidence predict outcomes across speeds and failure modes?

Task-Conditioned Worlds

Edit geometry, appearance, trajectories, camera response, and events against flight metrics rather than image fidelity alone.

Timing and Dynamics

Model exposure, bandwidth, estimator delay, inference jitter, actuator lag, saturation, and aerodynamic residuals together.

Predictive Evidence

Pair simulation, HiL, mixed reality, and flight using matched seeds, failure recall, rank correlation, and uncertainty.

Challenge focus

Research Questions

Specific open problems, not a survey-style mini-conference.

01

Task-Conditioned Edits

Which visual, geometric, dynamic-object, and sensor edits change time-to-contact, collision margin, tracking error, or policy actions?

02

Timing and Sensing

Which exposure, event bandwidth, estimator delay, inference jitter, control-rate, and compute budgets survive higher speed?

03

Coupled Transfer

How can visual mismatch be separated from actuator lag, aerodynamic residuals, disturbances, and estimator drift?

04

Predictive Evaluation

What rank correlation, paired failure recall, risk calibration, and uncertainty make sim, HiL, or MR predictive of flight?

05

Failure Sets and Artifacts

How should falsification find boundaries, and which seeds, assets, timestamps, trajectories, and negative results must be released?

Active participation

Workshop Format

Every block produces input for the public post-workshop artifact.

Evidence Cards

Contributors pre-register the claim, speed, policy interface, mismatch axes, failure, evaluation stage, and artifacts.

Claim-Evidence Clinic

Two to three spotlights lead into 15 posters; assigned speaker/PC mentors annotate each evidence card.

Protocol Sprints

Four groups draft testable criteria with facilitator, scribe, skeptic, and reporter roles.

Fishbowl and Red Team

An open rotating seat, recorded dissent, and anonymous adopt/revise/needs-evidence votes turn discussion into decisions.

Contributed papers

Call for Contributions

We will solicit and accept contributed workshop papers.

OpenReview will accept 2 to 4 page non-archival preliminary work, negative findings, datasets, benchmarks, systems, failure analyses, sim/real correlation studies, and positions. Each submission states its claim, speed regime, evaluation ladder, mismatch axes, failure evidence, and artifacts.

  • At least two conflict-checked reviews; a third resolves substantial disagreement.
  • Rubric: challenge fit, technical clarity, evidence, discussion value, and artifact potential.
  • Approximately 15 posters; 2-3 spotlights selected for technical complementarity and broad representation, not score alone.
  • Rigorous in-progress and negative results are welcome; every accepted author joins a protocol station.
  • Main CoRL 2026 papers will not be accepted as workshop papers.
